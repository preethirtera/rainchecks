The idea

Overcommitment happens in two seconds

It's the moment between reading a message and typing "yes!!". Every calendar starts working after you've already agreed. RainChecks inserts a deliberate pause before the yes — and makes the calendar the bad guy so you don't have to be.

The commitment inbox

Asks land here first, not on your calendar. Paste "dinner thurs 7 w/ maya" and the who, when, and hours are parsed for you. No manual time entry, ever.

The yes-budget

You set a weekly cap of social hours. Every decision shows the running total at the moment it matters: "saying yes puts this week at 84%."

The sleep-on-it timer

One tap copies "That sounds so fun! Let me check my calendar and text you" and schedules the reminder to actually decide.

The graceful no

Declines in your voice — including counter-offers pulled from evenings you're actually free.

The forecast

Your week, measured in weather

The budget isn't a progress bar — it's a sky. The fuller your week, the worse the forecast, and the ambient rain in the app thickens with it. Cross 100% and lightning strikes.

The rules that hold

Software that won't let you people-please

🔒

The 24-hour rule

Weekend trips, full-day events, helping someone move: for big asks, the Yes button physically locks for a cooling-off period you choose. You can raincheck instantly, you can sleep on it instantly — agreeing on the spot is the one thing the app refuses to do.

💜

Alone time is real plans

Protected blocks count against your budget, trigger conflict warnings, and the app's language never reveals they're solo. If someone asks, you already have plans. Full stop.

🥀

The flake tax

Backing out of a commitment doesn't refund the hours — your week already paid for it. The plan sits struck-through on your list, the flake goes on your record, and yes, the lightning strikes. Saying no early is free; breaking a yes costs.

⚠️

Conflict heads-up

A new ask that collides with anything you've committed to — including alone time — triggers an instant "you already have plans at that time" notification, plus overload warnings like "that would make it four days in a row."

Saying no, beautifully

Replies in your voice, blame on the app

Pick a tone — or write your own declines, which always appear first. Counter-offers name evenings you're actually free, checked against your real week. Everything copies to your clipboard in one tap.

Gentle
That sounds so fun! But this week is already packed for me. Can we find another time soon? 💜
Firm
I can't make it, my week is already committed.
Snarky
RainChecks the app has denied this request on my behalf. take it up with the app
Counter-offer
That day doesn't work for me, but I'd love to see you Saturday! 💜

And the Stats tab keeps score for you: hours reclaimed by saying no, your weeks-under-budget streak, flakes on record, and a Sunday look back at last week's yeses — tap the ones you regretted and the app suggests a saner budget.

Privacy is the architecture

Almost nothing to permit

RainChecks was designed backwards from "no creepy permissions." Instead of pulling your data in, you push things in — and everything lives on your device.
